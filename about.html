Our mission

Wearables give you data. We wanted one that gives you answers.

PulseBand exists because every one of us owned a tracker that buried a useful signal in a dashboard nobody had time to read. So we built the band we wanted: fewer numbers, more plain-English guidance.

How we started

Five friends, one shared spreadsheet of missed step goals.

We prototyped the first PulseBand on a kitchen table in 2024, wiring a heart-rate sensor to a strip of silicone. Eighteen months and three hardware revisions later, it's shipping to the people who pre-ordered it.
